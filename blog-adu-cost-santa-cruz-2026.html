What Drives Costs Up on the Central Coast?

Santa Cruz County has some of the highest construction costs in California. Here are the main factors:

  • Labor costs — Skilled trades command premium wages in this market
  • Permit fees — Santa Cruz County fees can add $15,000–$25,000 to your project
  • Soils & geology — Many properties require special foundations due to soil conditions
  • Utility connections — Hooking up water, sewer, and electrical can add $20,000+ on older properties
  • Site access — Narrow lots, steep grades, or difficult access increase labor costs
  • Materials — Supply chain costs remain elevated post-pandemic

How to Get the Most Accurate Quote

The most accurate ADU cost comes from a site visit. Every property is different — lot size, soil conditions, utility locations, existing structures, and HOA rules all affect pricing. At Peninsula Construction, our initial consultation is free and includes a property walkthrough and preliminary budget range.

We also offer three design options for 2-bedroom ADUs under 750 square feet, so you can choose the layout that fits your goals and your budget before construction begins.

Is an ADU Worth the Investment?

In most cases on the Central Coast — yes, absolutely. Rental rates in Santa Cruz and Monterey Counties are among the highest in the country. A well-built 1-bedroom ADU can rent for $2,200–$3,000 per month. At those rates, a $200,000 ADU pays for itself in 6–8 years — and adds significant value to your property the moment it's permitted.
