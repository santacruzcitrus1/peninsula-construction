The Peninsula Construction Process

We've built hundreds of projects over 30+ years and refined our process to be smooth, transparent, and predictable. No surprises, no runaround — just clear communication from start to finish.

Most ADU projects complete in 6–9 months. Here's exactly what that looks like.

Typical Timeline

📋 Consultation & Design: 1–2 months

🏛️ Permitting: 2–4 months

🔨 Construction: 3–5 months

✅ Final Inspection: 1–2 weeks

1

Free Consultation

Richard visits your property personally to assess feasibility, understand your goals, and give you honest feedback on what's possible. You'll leave with a clear sense of scope, timeline, and budget — no obligation.

Richard handles this himself. Not a sales rep.
2

Design & Planning

We create architectural drawings tailored to your lot, goals, and budget. You choose from our standard floor plans or customize. We review everything together before moving to permits.

3 standard 2BR designs under 750 sq ft available — faster permitting, proven layouts.
3

Permit Processing

We handle all city and county permit applications, structural engineering submissions, and utility coordination. You don't deal with city hall — we do that for you.

California ADU laws now require cities to approve complete ADU applications within 60 days.
4

Site Preparation

Grading, foundation, utility stub-outs — we prep the site properly so construction runs smoothly. Good site prep is the difference between a project that finishes on time and one that doesn't.

5

Construction

Our crew builds your ADU with Richard actively on-site. You get regular updates and Richard is always reachable. We don't disappear for weeks at a time.

Framing → Rough mechanical → Insulation → Drywall → Finishes → Final trim
6

Final Inspection & Delivery

We schedule and pass all final city inspections, complete the punch list, do a full site cleanup, and hand you the keys. Your ADU is move-in ready and legally permitted.

We also provide your after-build property value assessment — see your ROI in black and white.
